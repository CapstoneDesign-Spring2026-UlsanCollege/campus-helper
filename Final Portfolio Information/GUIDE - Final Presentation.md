# Final Presentation Guide

## Capstone Design — Spring 2026

## Date

Presentations begin:

**Wednesday, June 17, 2026, at 10:00 AM**

## Maximum Time

**15 minutes per team**

## Slides

Slides are optional.

There is no slide minimum or maximum.

Use slides only when they help your audience understand the project.

## Required Materials

Inside:

```txt
portfolio/07-final-presentation/
```

include:

```txt
FINAL_PRESENTATION_SCRIPT.md
TECHNICAL_DEFENSE_PREP.md
BACKUP_DEMO.md
slides/
```

Include slides when used.

When practical, include:

- editable slide link,
- PDF export,
- presentation script,
- backup screenshots,
- backup video,
- demo notes,
- and technical-defense notes.

## Recommended Time Split

| Segment | Time | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| Project story | 2 min | User, problem, solution, final MVP |
| Live MVP demo | 5 min | Core working flow |
| Semester journey | 2 min | Major changes, cuts, and improvements |
| QA and engineering evidence | 2 min | Bugs, tests, docs, deployment, reliability |
| AI use and code ownership | 1 min | AI help and human verification |
| Technical defense | 3 min | Instructor questions |
| **Maximum** | **15 min** |  |

## Suggested Speaking Roles

| Segment | Suggested role |
|---|---|
| Project story | PM or opening presenter |
| Live MVP demo | Demo Driver |
| Semester journey | PM or Scribe |
| QA and evidence | QA Lead |
| AI use and ownership | AI Steward |
| Technical defense | Any student selected |

Students may combine roles. Every student should be ready to speak.

## Presentation Rules

- Show the real project.
- Show the core user flow.
- Be honest about bugs.
- Use the submitted portfolio.
- Link evidence.
- Protect time for technical defense.
- Rehearse before presentation day.
- Test your backup.
- Do not add last-minute features that break the app.
- Do not spend most of the time on background information.
- Do not hide behind slides.

## Backup Plan

Your team chooses the backup plan.

Possible options:

- recorded demo,
- screenshots,
- local working version,
- seeded test data,
- second laptop,
- screen recording,
- click-through fallback,
- or documented alternative flow.

Your team is responsible for making the presentation work.

## What Good Looks Like

- Clear problem and user.
- Focused final MVP.
- Reliable core demo.
- Visible engineering evidence.
- Honest bugs and limitations.
- Clear AI-use disclosure.
- Students who can explain the work.
- Presentation ends on time.

## Common Mistakes

- Too much project background.
- Demo starts without testing login, accounts, or data.
- Slides are polished but app is not ready.
- One person speaks for the entire team.
- Bugs are hidden.
- No backup exists.
- Technical-defense time disappears.
- Team members cannot explain their own evidence.
